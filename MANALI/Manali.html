Manali

About

Manali is a resort town, near Kullu town in Kullu district in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh.It is situated in the northern end of the Kullu Valley, formed by the Beas River. The town is located in the Kullu district, approximately 270 kilometres (170 mi) north of the state capital of Shimla and 544 kilometres (338 mi) northeast of the national capital of New Delhi.

Mythology

Manali is named after Manu, the progenitor of humanity in Hinduism. The name Manali is regarded as the derivative of Manu-Alaya (transl,the abode of Manu').[4] In Hindu cosmology, Manu is believed to have stepped off his ark in Manali to recreate human life after a great flood had deluged the world at the end of an cyclic age.

Manali, nestled in the Himalayas, is known for its rich tradition of handicrafts, which reflect the culture and heritage of Himachal Pradesh. Here are some of the most famous handicrafts of Manali:

  • Wood Carvings
  • Tibetian Handicrafts
